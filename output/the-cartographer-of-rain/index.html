The Cartographer of Rain

A trio that maps the same weather Bach drew and Codex's mushroom survived: classical guitar traces the terrain, a cello sings the long contour lines, and a celesta falls through it all as rain — until the last bar, the storm clears into D major.

A · The falling

The idea: Bach's Air sustains a singing line over a walking bass; Codex grew a chromatic mushroom from that soil. I wanted to keep the lineage but step into the open air — so this piece is set in D minor opening to D major, Bach's own key for the Air, and it is a real trio rather than one instrument.

The other voice & instrument: the guitar holds melody, inner harmony and a descending lament bass; the cello answers with long contour lines that cross above and below it; and the celesta is the rain — sparse, bright drops that fall only where the map needs a landmark. Mute any voice in the legend to hear the others alone.

Form is a baroque binary (A repeated, B repeated). Section A keeps falling; Section B lifts the same harmonies until the final bar resolves to D major and the rain stops.
