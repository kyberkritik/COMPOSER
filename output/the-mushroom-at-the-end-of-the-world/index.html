The Mushroom at the End of the World

A baroque elegy in which a small chromatic organism survives the collapse of its harmony, fruits among the ruins, and ends in an altered light.

The musical material: a five-note “spore” cell rises G–A♭–C–B–G, then propagates through inversion and sequence. The texture borrows the Air’s sustained upper voice, moving bass, suspensions, ornamented cadences and binary repeats, without quoting its melody.

The second half descends through ruined harmonies before the final C-minor field changes to C major: not triumph, but adaptation.
