Welt am Draht

If Camel had written it inside a cold simulation: a lyrical lead and Mellotron warmth threaded through a high, melodic Joy-Division bass and motorik toms. Minimal, modal, and sung by two synthesized voices as a sketch of where the real ones would go.

The harmony: a modal D-minor / Dorian world. The verses sit dark on a D pedal (Joy Division), the choruses lift through F–C–B♭ and a bright Dorian G before the leading-tone A pulls home (Camel). Nothing is crowded — the effect is in the few notes that are chosen.

The instruments: a high, singing bass carries melody the way Peter Hook would; motorik toms and a gated snare drive it; a Mellotron pad and Rhodes give the warmth; and a lyrical lead takes the instrumental bridge like Andrew Latimer. A male baritone sings the verses and a female voice floats the choruses — both synthesized from formants, a sketch of the real vocal.
